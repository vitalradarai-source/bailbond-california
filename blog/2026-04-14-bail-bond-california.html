California in Detail — What Makes This City's Bail Cases Different

California bail bonds operate under a specific legal framework that differs from many other states. California Insurance Code § 1800.4 sets the bail premium at 10% of the face bail amount — this is the rate for every licensed agent in the state. The rate is not negotiable in the sense of the percentage, but payment plans are available for qualifying clients and some agents offer discounts tied to specific circumstances (cosigner with employment, etc.).

The California system assigns bail through two mechanisms: county bail schedules (used at booking, before arraignment) and judicial discretion (set by the judge at arraignment under Penal Code § 1269). The county schedule amount is what applies at the jail in the first 24–48 hours after arrest. If a bond is posted against that amount, the defendant is released pending arraignment. If bond is not posted before arraignment, the judge can raise, lower, or deny bail under California Constitution Article I, Section 12.

Timing is everything. Penal Code § 825 requires arraignment within 48 hours of arrest (excluding Sundays and court holidays). Posting bail before arraignment locks in the scheduled amount. Waiting can mean a higher judicial bail, or — in serious cases — no bail at all. The California system has no mandatory overnight hold; the "72-hour hold" claim that sometimes appears on other websites is simply incorrect under California law. Bail can be posted immediately once booking is complete.

Typical Late-Night Arrest Scenarios in California

A California DUI arrest (VC 23152) with bail scheduled at $15,000 — 10% premium is $1,500 paid to the bondsman, who posts the full $15,000 with the jail as a surety bond backed by a licensed California insurer.

A California domestic violence arrest (PC 273.5) with bail scheduled at $50,000 — 10% is $5,000. A PC 136.2 criminal protective order will likely issue at arraignment, restricting contact with the alleged victim while the case is pending.

A California felony case approaching the 48-hour arraignment deadline under PC § 825 — posting at the scheduled amount before arraignment locks in the bail. Missing that window gives the judge full discretion to modify or deny bail.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does a California arrest actually get processed?

California bail bonds operate at a 10% premium under Insurance Code § 1800.4. Bail is set by county schedules at booking and can be modified at arraignment (Penal Code § 1269). There is no mandatory overnight hold — bail can be posted immediately after booking is complete.

How much does a bail bond actually cost in California?

The bail bond fee is 10% of the total bail amount under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. For a first-offense DUI with bail set at $15,000, you pay $1,500. That 10% is our fee for posting the full bond — it is not refunded at case conclusion. Payment plans are available for qualifying clients.

Can California bail be posted the same night?

Yes, in most cases. California does not impose a mandatory overnight hold. Most California DUI and misdemeanor arrests result in same-night or early-morning release.
