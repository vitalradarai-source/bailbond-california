Multi-County Situations

Arrested in a Different County?

California has 58 counties, each with its own jail system, bail schedule, and court procedures. When someone is arrested in a county they don't live in, the situation gets more confusing — but we handle it every day.

You post bail in the county of arrest, not the county of residence. If your son lives in San Diego but was arrested in LA County, we post bail at the LA County facility. The case will be heard in LA County Superior Court. However, the defendant can petition to transfer the case after arraignment in limited circumstances.

We have relationships with facilities in every major California county. No matter where the arrest happened, one call to (626) 478-1062 gets the process started — even if it's a county 300 miles away.
